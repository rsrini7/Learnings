# J.P. Morgan on Blockchain – Strategy, Timeline, and Business Outcomes

## 1. Executive Snapshot

**Core takeaway:** J.P. Morgan is not “doing crypto”; it is rebuilding institutional finance on blockchain rails – from internal settlement to on-chain bank money, tokenized assets, and capital markets issuance – with a regulator-first, institutional-only strategy.

> J.P. Morgan is using blockchain to modernize how money, assets, and capital markets operate—starting with internal settlement (JPM Coin), extending real bank money on-chain (JPMD), tokenizing yield-bearing assets (MONY), and now issuing real debt instruments on public blockchains. Rather than embracing crypto speculation, JPMC is methodically rebuilding institutional finance on blockchain rails while maintaining regulatory control.

---

## 2. J.P. Morgan’s Blockchain Philosophy

**How JPMC thinks about blockchain:**

- **Infrastructure, not speculation:** Blockchain is treated as a new *financial market infrastructure* layer, not as a new asset class for trading.
- **Regulator-first:** Everything is designed to fit into existing regulatory and bank-supervisory frameworks: deposits, securities, funds, and payments.
- **Institutional-only:** Built for large corporates, financial institutions, and asset managers – not for retail.
- **Focus area:** Money, settlement, collateral, and capital markets – not trading, memes, or public DeFi.

This philosophy explains why all JPMC initiatives map cleanly into the four buckets below, each targeting a specific financial function.

---

## 3. The Four Strategic Buckets

J.P. Morgan’s blockchain strategy can be understood as four distinct but connected layers:

1. **Internal Bank Settlement** – modernizing JPMC’s own plumbing
2. **On-chain Bank Money** – real deposit money represented on public chains
3. **Tokenized Investment Assets** – funds and RWAs that earn yield, used as collateral
4. **Tokenized Capital Markets** – issuance and lifecycle of debt and securities on-chain

Each bucket has different products, regulatory treatment, and business value.

---

## 4. Bucket 1 – Internal Bank Settlement (Plumbing)

### 4.1 Product: JPM Coin

**What it is**

- An **internal settlement token** representing U.S. dollars (and other currencies) on J.P. Morgan’s own permissioned blockchain infrastructure (Onyx / Kinexys stack).
- Used **only** by J.P. Morgan and permissioned institutional clients.

**Purpose**

- Enable **instant settlement** of payments between J.P. Morgan and its clients.
- Replace slower, batch-based internal ledger reconciliations with real-time atomic movements.
- Improve intraday liquidity management and reduce operational friction.

**What it is NOT**

- Not a public token
- Not tradable on exchanges
- Not “crypto” in the retail or speculative sense

**Mapping**

```text
Internal Banking Infrastructure
 └─ JPM Coin (internal settlement token)
```

**Business outcomes (indicative):**

- Material reduction in internal reconciliation times (hours → near real-time).
- Better intraday liquidity visibility for corporate treasurers.
- Forms the **base layer** on which the rest of JPMC’s blockchain strategy is built.

---

## 5. Bucket 2 – On-Chain Bank Money (Real Money on Blockchain)

### 5.1 Product: JPMD (Deposit Token)

**What it is**

- A **tokenized bank deposit**: fully backed 1:1 by U.S. dollar deposits at J.P. Morgan.
- Issued on **public blockchains** (starting with Coinbase’s Base/L2) with **permissioned access** for institutional users.

**Purpose**

- Enable **on-chain settlement** for institutional financial flows:
  - Wholesale payments
  - Trading and clearing of tokenized assets
  - On-chain cash leg for capital markets and collateral use cases
- Provide **24×7 programmable institutional money** that behaves like a deposit but settles like a crypto token.

**Key distinction**

- **JPMD is *commercial bank money*** – a claim on J.P. Morgan as a regulated bank.
- It is *not* a typical stablecoin issued by a non-bank; it sits clearly under bank regulation and balance-sheet treatment.

**Competitive positioning**

- Competes with:
  - Traditional payment rails (wires, SWIFT, ACH) for institutional flows
  - USDC/USDT **for institutional, regulated use cases**, especially where counterparty/regulatory risk of non-bank issuers is a concern.

**Mapping**

```text
Commercial Bank Money (On-chain)
 └─ JPMD (Deposit Token – public chain, institutional access)
```

**Business outcomes (directional):**

- New fee and services revenue around **on-chain settlement** and cash management.
- Increased stickiness of large institutional clients using JPMC rails across both TradFi and on-chain ecosystems.
- Positions JPMC as a key settlement bank for **tokenized markets** and public blockchain activity.

## 6. Bucket 3 – Tokenized Investment Assets (Yield, Not Money)

### 6.1 Product: MONY (My OnChain Net Yield Fund)

**What it is**

- A **tokenized money market fund (MMF)** issued by J.P. Morgan Asset Management.
- Runs on **public Ethereum** as a tokenized representation of a traditional MMF share.
- Underlying portfolio:
  - Short-term U.S. Treasuries
  - Treasury-backed repos and highly liquid short-duration instruments

**Purpose**

- Provide **on-chain yield-bearing collateral** for institutional users:
  - Corporate treasuries parking cash in yield-generating instruments
  - Use as collateral in tokenized financing and derivatives
  - Potential composability with other institutional DeFi-like platforms (under strict permissions)

**Key clarification**

- MONY is an **investment security**, not money.
- It is **not**:
  - A deposit
  - Cash equivalent in the bank-money sense
  - A payment instrument

**Mapping**

```text
Tokenized Real-World Assets (RWA)
 └─ Tokenized Funds
     └─ MONY (Tokenized Money Market Fund)
```

**Business outcomes (directional):**

- New AUM channels from **on-chain native treasuries and funds** that prefer tokenized fund shares.
- High-margin fee revenue via asset management plus differentiation as a **tokenization-ready asset manager**.
- Provides high-quality collateral for JPMC’s broader tokenization and repo/derivatives strategy.

## 7. Bucket 4 – Tokenized Capital Markets (Debt & Securities)

### 7.1 Example: Tokenized Commercial Paper (Galaxy / Solana)

**What happened**

- J.P. Morgan **arranged** a **50M USD** commercial paper issuance for Galaxy.
- The instrument was issued and represented on the **Solana** public blockchain.
- **USDC** was used for settlement of the cash leg.
- Buyers included a mix of **crypto-native** and **traditional finance** institutions.

**Why this matters**

- This is **real-world debt**, not a pilot with toy amounts:
  - Real issuer (Galaxy)
  - Real debt obligation
  - Real investors
  - Public blockchain infrastructure
- Demonstrates JPMC’s ability to:
  - Structure and arrange tokenized debt
  - Plug into public chains (beyond its own private infrastructure)
  - Coordinate with stablecoin and RWA ecosystems

**JPMC’s role**

- **Lead arranger / structurer**, using its capital markets expertise.
- Not a crypto exchange, not an issuer of the stablecoin, and not running a public DeFi platform.

**Mapping**

```text
Tokenized Capital Markets
 └─ Tokenized Debt
     └─ Commercial Paper on Solana (arranged by J.P. Morgan)
```

**Business outcomes (directional):**

- Strengthens JPMC’s role as a **trusted arranger** for tokenized issuances across chains.
- Opens fee pools in **structuring, distribution, and lifecycle services** for tokenized debt and securities.
- Validates operational workflows for future large-scale tokenized bond and commercial paper markets.

## 8. Unified Stack – How the Pieces Fit Together

Conceptually, J.P. Morgan is building a **vertical stack** from money to assets to markets:

```text
┌───────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Tokenized Investment Assets               │
│  └─ MONY (MMF – yield-bearing, collateral)│
└───────────────────────────────────────────┘
┌───────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Tokenized Capital Markets                 │
│  └─ Tokenized Commercial Paper / Bonds    │
└───────────────────────────────────────────┘
┌───────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ On-Chain Bank Money                       │
│  └─ JPMD (Deposit Token)                  │
└───────────────────────────────────────────┘
┌───────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Internal Bank Settlement                  │
│  └─ JPM Coin                              │
└───────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Layer roles**

- **JPM Coin** – optimizes *internal and client* settlement across JPMC books.
- **JPMD** – provides a **public-chain-settled bank money** instrument for institutional flows.
- **MONY** – offers **yield-bearing collateral** and on-chain investment exposure.
- **Tokenized capital markets** – orchestrates **issuance, trading, and settlement** for securities and debt.

The strategic intent: any future institutional capital markets flow that touches public or private blockchains can **use J.P. Morgan rails, money, and assets end-to-end**.

## 9. Timeline of J.P. Morgan’s Blockchain Journey (2018–2025)

### 9.1 Milestone Timeline (High-Level)

```text
2018 – 2019
- Conception and early rollout of JPM Coin for internal settlement on permissioned blockchain.

2020
- Launch of Onyx as J.P. Morgan’s dedicated blockchain and digital assets unit.
- Expansion of JPM Coin use cases with select institutional clients.

2021 – 2022
- Scaling of Onyx platforms for payments, intraday repo, and DvP experiments.
- Early tokenization pilots with bonds and other securities.

2023
- Onyx infrastructure usage grows; JPMC positions itself publicly as a tokenization leader.
- Internal systems and client rails are hardened for production-grade volumes.

2024
- Strategic clients start using JPMC rails for **tokenized collateral and repo**.
- Tokenization of funds and money market instruments is prioritized.

2025
- **JPMD (deposit token)** launched on a major public L2 (e.g., Base).
- **MONY** tokenized MMF launched on Ethereum with institutional access.
- J.P. Morgan arranges **50M USD tokenized commercial paper** for Galaxy on Solana.
- Clear shift from pilots → **live capital markets and cash products on public chains**.
```

### 9.2 Visual Timeline (from the presentation)

- 2018–2020: **Infrastructure build-out** – JPM Coin; Onyx; internal use.
- 2021–2023: **Scaling and experimentation** – repo, DvP, early tokenization.
- 2024–2025: **Productization and public-chain expansion** – JPMD, MONY, tokenized debt.

## 10. Business Impact and Outcomes

### 10.1 Direct Outcomes

- **Operational efficiency**
  - Faster internal and client settlement, reducing reconciliation workloads and operational risk.
  - Improved intraday liquidity and collateral mobility.

- **New revenue lines**
  - Fees from on-chain settlement services (JPMD flows).
  - Asset management fees from tokenized funds (MONY and future products).
  - Capital markets fees for tokenized issuance, distribution, and lifecycle services.

- **Balance sheet & funding advantages**
  - Stickier institutional deposits via JPMD and integrated cash + tokenization services.
  - Ability to integrate funding, collateral, and trading into unified digital workflows.

### 10.2 Strategic Outcomes

- **Ecosystem positioning**
  - JPMC is becoming a **horizontal layer** across:
    - On-chain cash (JPMD)
    - On-chain collateral (MONY and future RWAs)
    - On-chain markets (tokenized debt and securities)
  - Enables JPMC to be the **trusted intermediary** between TradFi and public blockchains.

- **Regulatory comfort**
  - By using bank deposits, regulated funds, and existing capital markets frameworks, JPMC keeps regulators engaged and comfortable.
  - This is the opposite of the “launch first, ask forgiveness later” playbook of many crypto-native projects.

- **Client stickiness and defensibility**
  - Large corporates and institutions integrating with JPMC’s blockchain infrastructure face high switching costs.
  - Over time, JPMC becomes not just a bank, but a **core transaction and settlement OS** for institutional blockchain activity.

## 12. What J.P. Morgan Is *Not* Trying to Do

JPMC is **deliberately avoiding**:

- ❌ Competing with retail crypto exchanges
- ❌ Issuing retail stablecoins for the public
- ❌ Enabling open, permissionless DeFi for retail users
- ❌ Replacing or “killing” public blockchains

Instead, it aims to:

- ✅ Use public blockchains **selectively**, where they add value
- ✅ Maintain **permissioned access** and strong KYC/AML
- ✅ Preserve **regulatory clarity** by fitting products into existing legal categories
